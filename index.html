How to use our products

Plain-language walkthroughs for every tool we make. No tech jargon — just the steps that get you from start to finish.

QuickClips

Upload a video → get short clips ready to post in minutes

Try Free →
1

Create your free account

Go to myquickclips.com and click Get Started Free. Enter your email, create a password, and you're in — no credit card needed. The whole signup takes about 60 seconds.

2

Choose how to upload your video

Click Upload in the left sidebar. You'll see two options:

  • Clip It with AI — best for long videos. AI will watch your video and pick the best 15–90 second moments for social media automatically.
  • Upload Entire Video — best for videos you've already trimmed. Goes straight to the editor so you can add captions, music, and post it yourself.

Pick whichever fits your situation and click Choose File to select a video from your phone or computer.

3

Wait for processing (usually 2–5 minutes)

If you chose Clip It, you'll see a progress screen while our AI analyzes your video. It's finding the most engaging moments and writing captions for each one. You can close the tab and come back — it keeps running in the background.

If you chose Upload Entire Video, it'll take under a minute and open the editor automatically.

4

Review your clips

Head to Clips in the sidebar. You'll see thumbnail cards for every clip the AI found. Each one shows a Viral Score (how likely it is to perform well) and a short summary. Click any clip to see more details, including ready-to-use captions for TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitter, and Facebook.

5

Edit in the video editor (optional)

Click Open in Editor on any clip to fine-tune it. Inside the editor you can:

  • Trim the start and end by dragging the handles on the timeline
  • Add animated captions — choose from 5 styles (PopBold, Karaoke, Neon, and more)
  • Add background music from our library or upload your own
  • Record a voiceover directly in the browser
  • Adjust the video speed (slow motion, 1.5×, 2×)
  • Change the aspect ratio for different platforms (9:16 for TikTok, 16:9 for YouTube)

Everything saves automatically. Click Export when you're happy with it.

6

Publish or schedule to social media

In the Publish tab inside the editor, choose your platform, pick the AI-written caption (or write your own), and click Publish Now or Schedule. Your clip goes live without ever leaving QuickClips. Check the Calendar tab to see all your scheduled posts in one place.

💡 Tip: The best videos to upload are interviews, podcasts, YouTube videos, or any footage with someone talking — the AI picks out the strongest quotes and moments automatically.

ClipHook

Generate hooks and captions for any video while you browse

Install Free →
1

Install the Chrome extension

Go to getcliphook.com and click Add to Chrome — It's Free. Chrome will ask you to confirm — click Add Extension. A small green dot icon will appear in the top-right corner of your browser. That's ClipHook, ready to use.

2

Go to any YouTube, TikTok, or LinkedIn page

Browse normally. ClipHook works on YouTube videos, TikTok posts, LinkedIn articles, and X/Twitter posts. Navigate to any piece of content you want to create hooks or captions for.

3

Click the ClipHook icon

Click the green dot icon in your browser toolbar. A side panel slides open on the right side of your screen. ClipHook automatically reads the page — the video title, description, and content — so you don't have to type anything.

4

Choose what to generate

Pick one of four options:

  • Hooks — attention-grabbing opening lines for your post or video
  • Captions — full post captions with a hook, body, and call-to-action
  • Titles — click-worthy video or post titles
  • Hashtags — three sets of hashtags (high-volume, medium, and niche)

Click the button and you'll get 3–5 variations in about 3 seconds.

5

Copy and use it

Click Copy next to any result and paste it into TikTok, YouTube Studio, LinkedIn, or wherever you're posting. Done. No switching between apps, no writing prompts, no starting from scratch.

💡 Tip: Use ClipHook while browsing your competitors' videos or content in your niche — it's a great way to generate ideas for your own posts based on what's already working.

SponsorKit

Track every brand deal from first email to paid invoice

Try Free →
1

Sign up and set your rate card

Go to app.mysponsorkit.com and create a free account. In Settings → Rate Card, enter your standard rates for different types of content (YouTube dedicated video, TikTok, Instagram story, etc.). SponsorKit uses this to auto-fill your invoices.

2

Add a brand deal

When a brand reaches out, click New Deal and fill in: the brand name, the type of content, your rate, and the deadline. The deal appears as a card in your Pipeline — a visual board showing every deal by stage (Contacted → Negotiating → Contracted → Filming → Published → Paid).

3

Move deals through the pipeline

Drag deal cards from column to column as they progress. When a brand says yes, move it to Contracted. When you film, move it to Filming. When it's live, move it to Published. No more lost emails or forgotten follow-ups.

4

Send the invoice

When content is published, click Create Invoice on the deal card. SponsorKit fills in the brand name, amount, and your details automatically. Download it as a PDF and email it directly — or SponsorKit can send it for you with a payment link attached.

5

Track your earnings

The Analytics tab shows your total earnings, deals in progress, average deal size, and how much money is still owed to you. At tax time, every deal is already documented and totaled — no digging through Gmail.

💡 Tip: Even if a brand hasn't responded yet, add them to the pipeline in the Contacted stage. SponsorKit will remind you to follow up so no opportunity falls through the cracks.
